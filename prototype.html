How I work

My approach to building software

Not just what I build — how I think about building it.

01

Problem first, tech second

I don't pick a stack and look for a problem. I find a broken system, understand why it's broken, then choose the simplest tech that solves it. PostgreSQL over Elasticsearch when the query patterns are simple. FastAPI over Express when the pipeline is Python-native.

02

Ship, then polish

A deployed MVP with real users teaches more than a perfect prototype that never ships. I get to "works in production" fast, then iterate based on real usage. Every project on this page was live within weeks of starting.

03

AI as a force multiplier

I use AI the way a carpenter uses a power tool — it makes me faster, but the architecture decisions, the trade-offs, and the debugging are mine. I understand every line that ships. AI lets a solo builder ship what would normally take a team.

04

Own the full stack

From database schema to deployment pipeline, I own the entire lifecycle. Not because I have to — because understanding how layers connect makes you build better software. The best debugging happens when you can trace from the UI to the SQL query.
